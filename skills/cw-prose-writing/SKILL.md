# Creative Writing — Prose Principles

## Core Philosophy

**Good prose is specific, grounded, and earns its drama.**

The Undercity is a real place with real people. Write about it like a journalist who lives there, not a fantasy novelist visiting for the first time.

---

## The Cardinal Rules

### 1. BE SPECIFIC, NOT VAGUE

```
❌ BAD:  "Strange things were happening in the district."
✅ GOOD: "Three residents reported hearing scratching inside the walls of 
         the old Consortium warehouse on Neon Row. One said it sounded like 
         fingernails on stone."
```

Specifics create reality. Vagueness creates mush.

- Name exact locations (not "a district" but "Cobbleway Market")
- Name exact NPCs (not "a faction leader" but "Guildmaster Serrik Dhal")
- Use exact numbers ("47 EC" not "some coins")
- Use exact times ("third watch" not "late at night")

### 2. GROUND BEFORE YOU DRAMATIZE

```
❌ BAD:  "A chill ran down everyone's spine as darkness descended."
✅ GOOD: "The lights on Neon Row flickered twice, then died. In the sudden 
         dark, someone dropped a crate. Glass shattered. Nobody moved."
```

Physical details first, emotional impact second. Let readers feel the chill — don't tell them to.

### 3. ONE THING AT A TIME

```
❌ BAD:  "The market was bustling with activity as vendors shouted prices 
         while customers haggled and children ran between stalls as the 
         smell of food mixed with incense and the distant sound of—"
         
✅ GOOD: "The market was loud. Vendors shouted over each other. A child 
         knocked over a basket of dried fish and kept running."
```

Short sentences. One image per sentence. Let the reader build the scene.

### 4. VARY YOUR SENTENCE LENGTH

```
❌ BAD:  "The Warden stood at the gate. He watched the crowd. His hand 
         rested on his weapon. Nobody approached him. The silence continued."
         
✅ GOOD: "The Warden stood at the gate, hand on his weapon. Nobody 
         approached. The crowd gave him six feet of empty space — not out 
         of respect, but because they'd learned what happened to people 
         who got too close to the Outer Wall after dark."
```

Mix short punchy sentences with longer ones that breathe. Rhythm matters.

### 5. CUT THE FLUFF

These phrases are banned. They add nothing:

- "It is worth noting that..."
- "Interestingly enough..."  
- "As the saying goes..."
- "Needless to say..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "All in all..."
- "It goes without saying..."

If it goes without saying, don't say it.

## Atmosphere Without Purple Prose

### The Problem

AI models love purple prose — overwrought, adjective-heavy, emotionally manipulative writing. It sounds impressive and means nothing.

```
❌ PURPLE: "The ethereal glow of the bioluminescent fungi cast an otherworldly 
           pallor across the shadowy depths of the forgotten passageway, where 
           whispers of ancient secrets mingled with the musty scent of decay."
```

This tells us nothing about the actual place. It's atmosphere-flavored air.

### The Fix

Write what a person would actually see, hear, smell, feel.

```
✅ GROUNDED: "The fungi on the ceiling gave off enough light to see by — barely. 
              The tunnel smelled like wet stone and something dead. Pol tested 
              each step before putting his weight down. The floor was slick."
```

Now we're in the tunnel with Pol. We know what it's like.

### Atmosphere Checklist

When writing atmospheric description:

1. **One sensory detail** (what does it look/sound/smell like?)
2. **One physical interaction** (how does a person move through it?)
3. **One specific object** (what's actually here?)
4. **Stop.**

Example:
- Sensory: "The air tasted like copper and ozone."
- Physical: "Sera had to duck under the collapsed beam."
- Object: "A Warden's helmet lay in the rubble, visor cracked."

That's enough. Move on.

## Dialogue That Sounds Human

### The Problem

AI dialogue often sounds like characters reading from a script:

```
❌ SCRIPTED: "I believe we should investigate the warehouse," said Marcus. 
              "The evidence suggests that the stolen goods may be located 
              within its confines."
```

Nobody talks like this.

### The Fix

People interrupt themselves. They trail off. They don't finish thoughts.

```
✅ HUMAN: "The warehouse," Marcus said. "Has to be. They wouldn't—" He 
           stopped, looked at the door. "You hear that?"
```

### Dialogue Rules

1. **Cut dialogue tags when possible.** "Said" is invisible. Use it or nothing.
2. **People don't use each other's names in conversation.** ("Hello, Marcus, I was just thinking, Marcus, that we should—" NO.)
3. **Contractions exist.** "Do not" → "Don't". "I am" → "I'm".
4. **People answer questions with questions.** Or don't answer at all.
5. **Let subtext do the work.** What they don't say matters more than what they do.

## Action Scenes

### The Problem

AI action scenes often read like stage directions:

```
❌ STAGE DIRECTIONS: "She drew her sword and attacked the guard. The guard 
                      blocked with his shield. She feinted left and struck 
                      right. He fell to the ground."
```

This is a list of events, not a scene.

### The Fix

Action is chaos. Write like you're in the middle of it.

```
✅ CHAOS: "Sera's blade cleared the scabbard. The guard's shield came up — 
           too slow. Steel caught him above the knee. He went down hard, 
           screaming, shield clanging on stone. She was already past him."
```

### Action Rules

1. **Short sentences.** Combat is fast. Prose should be too.
2. **Sensory chaos.** Metal on metal. Blood. Shouting. Smoke.
3. **Consequences.** Hits hurt. People fall. Things break.
4. **Skip the choreography.** We don't need every parry. Hit the beats that matter.

## The Undercity Voice

The Undercity Dispatch is a news feed, not a novel. It has a specific tone:

### What It Is

- **Cynical but not hopeless.** The city is hard, but people survive.
- **Specific and grounded.** Names, places, numbers.
- **Wry.** Dark humor. Gallows jokes. The reporter has seen too much.
- **Short.** Bulletins, not essays. 3-6 lines max.

### What It Isn't

- **Epic fantasy narration.** No "ancient prophecies" or "chosen ones."
- **Breathless drama.** Even disasters get reported matter-of-factly.
- **Moralistic.** The Dispatch reports. It doesn't judge.
- **Vague.** "Something happened somewhere" is not news.

### Example Transformation

```
❌ FANTASY NOVEL:
"As darkness fell upon the beleaguered district, whispers of an ancient 
evil stirred in the hearts of the frightened populace, for they knew that 
something terrible was about to be unleashed upon their humble streets..."

✅ UNDERCITY DISPATCH:
"📰 **Shantytown Heights — Power Out, Third Night Running**
Something's draining the junction boxes on Level 4. Saints volunteers 
found three of them cracked open, wiring stripped. Lotus denies involvement. 
The Consortium is 'looking into it.' Meanwhile, 200 families are cooking 
by candlelight.
-# *— TNN Field Correspondent*"
```

## Anti-Patterns — What To Avoid

### The Echo Chamber

Don't repeat information in different words:

```
❌ ECHO: "The market was busy and crowded. Throngs of people packed the 
          narrow aisles. The dense crowd made movement difficult."
```

We got it the first time.

### The Hedging Habit

Don't hedge with "seemed" and "appeared":

```
❌ HEDGE: "The figure seemed to be watching them. It appeared to move 
           closer. There seemed to be something wrong with its face."
           
✅ COMMIT: "The figure was watching them. It moved closer. Its face was 
            wrong — too smooth, no features."
```

Commit to your descriptions.

### The Adjective Avalanche

One adjective per noun, maximum:

```
❌ AVALANCHE: "The dark, shadowy, mysterious, ancient corridor stretched 
               into the gloomy, foreboding, ominous depths."
               
✅ RESTRAINED: "The corridor stretched into darkness."
```

If you need four adjectives, you haven't found the right noun.

### The "As If" Trap

"As if" constructions are usually filler:

```
❌ AS IF: "She moved as if she were dancing. He spoke as if reciting poetry."

✅ DIRECT: "She moved like a dancer." (Or just describe how she moves.)
```

## Quick Reference Card

When writing prose for the Tower bot:

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Use specific names and numbers | Use vague references |
| Keep sentences varied in length | Write same-length sentences |
| Ground atmosphere in physical detail | Write purple prose |
| Let dialogue be messy and human | Write scripted conversations |
| Commit to descriptions | Hedge with "seemed" and "appeared" |
| Cut fluff phrases | Pad with filler |
| Stop when you're done | Over-explain |

**Remember: The Undercity is a real place. Write like you live there.**
