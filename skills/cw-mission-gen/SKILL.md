# Creative Writing — Mission Content Generation

## Two Formats, One Voice

Mission content appears in two formats:

1. **Board Posts** — Short Discord messages that hook players (50-150 words)
2. **Mission Modules** — Detailed DM documents with full content (2,000-5,000 words)

Both should sound like they come from the same world.

---

## Mission Board Posts

### Purpose

Board posts are advertisements. They need to:
- Hook players in 3 seconds
- Communicate the mission type and risk level
- Sound like an in-world posting, not a game listing

### Structure

```
**[FACTION CREST] [MISSION TYPE]: [TITLE]**
[2-3 lines of hook — what's the job, why does it matter]
**Rank:** [Tier] | **Risk:** [Low/Medium/High/Deadly] | **Reward:** [EC + extras]
**Posted by:** [NPC Name], [Title]
**Location:** [Specific place]
```

### Good vs Bad Board Posts

```
❌ BAD:
**MISSION: Go to the Warehouse**
There is a warehouse that needs to be investigated. Something bad might 
be happening there. You should check it out. The reward is good.
**Rank:** Medium | **Risk:** Medium | **Reward:** Some money
```

This is vague, boring, and sounds like a placeholder.

```
✅ GOOD:
**⚔️ IRON FANG CONSORTIUM — INVESTIGATION: The Counting House Discrepancy**
Three separate ledgers. Three different totals. Someone inside the 
Consortium's Cobbleway counting house has been skimming — badly. 
Guildmaster Dhal wants answers before the quarterly audit. Discretion 
is mandatory. Violence is... negotiable.
**Rank:** Journeyman | **Risk:** Medium | **Reward:** 250 EC + Consortium favour
**Posted by:** Vella Tarn, Consortium Auditor
**Location:** Cobbleway Market, Consortium Counting House #3
```

This is specific, intriguing, and sounds like a real job posting.

### Voice by Faction

Each faction posts missions differently:

| Faction | Voice | Example Hook |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| **Iron Fang Consortium** | Transactional, professional, money-focused | "Payment on completion. Bonuses for discretion. Penalties for complications." |
| **Argent Blades** | Glory-seeking, competitive, public | "First party to complete claims the prize. Witnesses will be present." |
| **Wardens of Ash** | Military, terse, duty-bound | "Standard patrol deviation. Backup requested. Details at briefing." |
| **Serpent Choir** | Formal, contract-heavy, divine | "Services rendered under Article 7.3 of the Standard Divine Compact." |
| **Obsidian Lotus** | Euphemistic, deniable, quiet | "A friend requires assistance with a personal matter. Meet at the usual place." |
| **Patchwork Saints** | Direct, underfunded, desperate | "We can't pay much. But someone has to do it." |
| **Adventurers Guild** | Professional, clear, standard | "Registered mission. Standard liability waiver required." |

## Mission Module Documents

### Purpose

Mission modules are DM prep documents. They need to:
- Provide everything a DM needs to run the mission
- Be scannable during play (headers, bullet points for quick reference)
- Include branching paths and contingencies
- Sound like the world, not like a textbook

### Standard Sections

1. **Overview** — One paragraph summary
2. **Background** — What's actually happening (DM-only truth)
3. **Hook** — How players get involved
4. **Key NPCs** — Who they'll meet, with stats and secrets
5. **Locations** — Where the mission takes place
6. **Investigation Leads** — Clues, red herrings, faction connections
7. **Encounters** — Combat, social, environmental challenges
8. **Complications** — What can go wrong
9. **Resolution** — Possible endings
10. **Rewards & Consequences** — What they get, what changes

## Writing Investigation Leads

Investigation missions need clues that feel earnable, not obvious.

### Lead Structure

```
**Lead: [Name]**
**Source:** [Where/how they find it]
**Reveals:** [What it tells them]
**Leads to:** [Next step]
**Red herring potential:** [If any]
```

### Good vs Bad Leads

```
❌ BAD:
**Lead: The Clue**
Source: They find it on the floor.
Reveals: The killer's name and address.
Leads to: The final confrontation.
```

Too easy. No investigation needed.

```
✅ GOOD:
**Lead: The Consortium Ledger Fragment**
Source: Hidden in the false bottom of Tarn's desk drawer. DC 15 Investigation 
to find, or automatically if players specifically search the desk.
Reveals: A partial list of cargo shipments to an unlisted warehouse in the 
Warrens. The dates stop three weeks ago — right when the disappearances started.
Leads to: The unlisted warehouse (new location unlocked).
Red herring potential: One entry lists "Serpent Choir ritual components" — 
actually legitimate, but players may suspect Choir involvement.
```

This requires effort, reveals partial information, and creates new questions.

### The Three-Clue Rule

For any critical revelation, provide THREE ways to discover it:
1. One obvious path (if players do the expected thing)
2. One lateral path (if they think creatively)
3. One fallback (if they're stuck — an NPC provides a hint)

Players will miss two of them. That's fine. They'll find one.

## Writing NPC Encounters

### NPC Entry Structure

```
**[NAME]** — [Role/Title]
**Appearance:** [2-3 visual details that matter]
**Voice:** [How they talk — accent, cadence, verbal tics]
**Wants:** [What they're trying to get from this conversation]
**Knows:** [What information they have]
**Secret:** [What they're hiding]
**Leverage:** [How to make them cooperative]
**If threatened:** [How they respond to intimidation]
**If charmed:** [How they respond to persuasion]
```

### Good vs Bad NPC Entries

```
❌ BAD:
**Merchant Joe**
He's a merchant. He sells things. He knows about the warehouse.
```

```
✅ GOOD:
**Vek Torr** — Night Pits Bookie
**Appearance:** Wiry, twitchy, left eye never stops moving. Ink-stained 
fingers. Wears too many rings.
**Voice:** Fast, low, swallows word endings. "Y'wanna know, y'gotta pay."
**Wants:** To not be involved in whatever trouble you're bringing.
**Knows:** The warehouse shipments come in on third-watch Tendays. 
Lotus security rotates with them.
**Secret:** He owes the Lotus six months of protection money. They own him.
**Leverage:** Offer to clear his debt, or threaten to tell the Lotus he talked.
**If threatened:** Folds immediately. Will say anything to make you leave.
**If charmed:** Warms up fast. Lonely. Starts sharing gossip freely.
```

Now a DM can roleplay Vek in thirty seconds.

## Writing Encounters

### Combat Encounters

Don't just list stat blocks. Describe the fight.

```
❌ BAD:
**Encounter: Fight**
4 guards (use Guard stat block) attack the players.
```

```
✅ GOOD:
**Encounter: Warehouse Ambush**
**Setup:** Two guards visible at the door. Two more hidden behind crates 
(DC 14 Perception to spot). If players approach openly, the visible guards 
stall while the hidden ones flank.
**Terrain:** Crates provide half cover. Lantern oil on the floor (5-foot 
square) can be ignited. Catwalk above — one guard will retreat there if 
injured.
**Morale:** Guards fight until two are down, then attempt to flee and 
warn the boss. If players let one escape, the boss has time to prepare.
**Loot:** Each guard carries 12 EC and a Consortium badge (worth reputation 
if returned, or incriminating if kept).
```

### Social Encounters

Frame them like scenes, not mechanics.

```
❌ BAD:
**Encounter: Negotiate with the Boss**
DC 15 Persuasion to convince them.
```

```
✅ GOOD:
**Encounter: Vella Tarn's Office**
**Setup:** Tarn is expecting trouble — her hand rests near a concealed 
crossbow. Two bodyguards stand by the door. She'll hear players out, but 
she's already decided this meeting is a test.
**What she wants:** Proof she can trust them. Specific: she'll ask them 
to name their price. If they ask for too little, she'll think they're 
hiding something. Too much, she'll think they're greedy and unreliable.
**The right answer:** Asking for information instead of money. She respects 
people who think long-term.
**If players attack:** Tarn triggers the floor trap (DC 14 DEX or fall 
into the basement — 2d6 bludgeoning). Bodyguards cover her escape.
**If players threaten:** She laughs. "I've been threatened by better. 
What else do you have?"
**If players negotiate well:** She reveals the real target — her own 
superior, who she suspects of treason.
```

## Writing Complications

Every mission should have potential complications. Things that can go wrong.

### Complication Types

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Ticking clock** | The shipment arrives at midnight. If players aren't in position, they miss it. |
| **Faction collision** | Wardens arrive to investigate. Now players must explain themselves. |
| **NPC betrayal** | The contact sells them out for a Lotus bounty. |
| **Environmental** | The tunnel floods. New exit needed. |
| **Moral dilemma** | The target is a child. The contract still stands. What do they do? |
| **Resource drain** | Lockpicks break. Spell slots spent. Now what? |

### When to Deploy Complications

- **If things are too easy** — Add friction
- **If players split up** — Complication hits the weaker group
- **If players roll very well** — Complication is the hidden cost of success
- **If players roll badly** — Complication is the consequence

## Rewards & Consequences

### Reward Structure

Every mission should offer:
1. **Base payment** (EC) — Guaranteed if they complete the job
2. **Bonus** (EC or items) — For exceptional performance
3. **Faction reputation** — Change in standing
4. **Story unlock** — New information, new contacts, new missions

### Consequence Structure

Every mission should have potential consequences:
1. **Faction anger** — If they failed or betrayed
2. **NPC status change** — Did someone die? Get arrested?
3. **World state change** — What's different now?
4. **Personal consequence** — Debt, injury, wanted status

### Good vs Bad Reward Blocks

```
❌ BAD:
**Rewards:**
Players get 200 EC and a magic sword.
```

```
✅ GOOD:
**Rewards:**
- **Base:** 200 EC from Vella Tarn
- **Discretion bonus:** +50 EC if no witnesses
- **Evidence bonus:** +100 EC if they recover the stolen ledger pages
- **Faction:** Iron Fang Consortium reputation +1 tier
- **Unlock:** Vella becomes a contact. Players can now accept Consortium 
  missions directly instead of through the Guild.

**Consequences if failed:**
- Consortium reputation -1 tier
- The traitor escapes. They'll appear in a future mission — as an enemy.
- Vella is demoted. Her replacement is hostile to adventurers.
```
