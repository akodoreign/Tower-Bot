FLOOR 3 — THE DAY THE WALL HELD
A Greyhawk Shard Scenario — Year Two of the War

DESIGN OVERVIEW (DM-FACING)

Tone: Desperate last stand / Tactical war movie / Tragic victory — all three, layered
Emotional Arc: One year of waiting — the moment of truth — costly, real, remembered victory
Tower Role: Observer only — this floor ends on the party's terms, not the Tower's
Setting: The border fort wall and the bay docks, simultaneous
War State: The horde has arrived. BLACKWAKE is two hours from the bay.
Split Action: Sir Alard holds the wall. The party takes a division to stop the landing.
Win Condition: Stop BLACKWAKE before it unloads. Survive until Alard breaks the siege.
Floor End: Victory — costly but real. The horde is stopped here.

One year has passed since the party stood in the swamp, watching an ironclad take shape in the fog. One year since they dropped proof on a war table and watched command's certainty crack.

The horde came on schedule. BLACKWAKE came with it.

The smart play is not the obvious one. Everyone else is at the wall. The party knows what the wall doesn't stop.

---

BLOCK 1 — THE RETURN
A Year Later. The War Is Here.

DM READ-ALOUD: Arrival

The Tower translates you mid-march. Again.

Same cold air. Same wet earth. Same smell of pitch and iron.

But the fort is different now.

The walls are taller. The stakes are doubled. The watchtowers have two soldiers where there used to be one.

And the sky to the east is wrong.

Too much smoke. Too much orange. Too low on the horizon to be sunrise.

A soldier near the gate sees you and his face does something complicated.

Not surprise. Not relief.

Recognition.

"They said you'd be back," he says. "He said you'd be back."

He doesn't explain who he means. He doesn't need to.

WHAT HAS CHANGED (ONE YEAR)

The party arrives to find the fort transformed — not by comfort, but by grim preparation. Sir Alard Montreval returned four months after the party left Floor 2. His terms were accepted, quietly, by officers who had no better options.

What he built in the intervening year:
- Mobile defense doctrine — the fort is no longer designed to hold ground, but to bleed the enemy and move
- Civilian evacuation routes — pre-planned, drilled, protected
- A division loyal to him personally, trained for independent action
- Bay watchers — scouts who have been tracking BLACKWAKE's progress for six weeks

What the party finds when they arrive:
- Alard at the command table, calm, giving final orders — he looks up when they enter and nods once
- The bay watcher report on the table: BLACKWAKE is two hours out
- The wall defense is organized. The dock defense is not — no one has been willing to pull forces from the wall

SIR ALARD MONTREVAL — FINAL BRIEFING

He does not make a speech. He has three minutes and he uses them like a surgeon.

"The wall will hold or it won't. I have done everything I can for it."

"The ship is the problem no one at this table wants to name."

"If BLACKWAKE reaches the bay and unloads, we fight on two fronts. We lose on two fronts."

"I need someone at the docks who has seen it. Who knows what it can do."

He looks at the party directly.

"You know what it can do."

He offers them a choice — clearly, without pressure:

STAY AT THE WALL: Fight alongside Alard. The wall defense is brutal and honorable. The party's combat power is real here.

GO TO THE DOCKS: Take a division of 40 soldiers. Stop BLACKWAKE before it lands. The smart play. The harder one.

DM NOTE: If the party chooses the wall, run a modified version of Block 2 as the wall defense, then pivot to Block 4 when BLACKWAKE appears in the bay and makes the dock defense necessary anyway. The smart play is going to the docks first. Reward it by making the wall defense outcome worse if they don't.

BLOCK 2 — THE WALL
Sir Alard's Fight (Background Track)

The party does not fight this battle. But they feel it. The wall is two miles behind them and it is never quiet.

WALL BEATS (Read at DM Discretion)

BEAT 1 — As the party reaches the dock road:
The horn sounds from the fort. One long note. The assault has begun. The sound of it carries further than it should.

BEAT 2 — Midway through the docks:
Smoke rises from the wall. Not from fire — from signal torches. Alard is holding, but he is burning his reserves to do it.

BEAT 3 — When BLACKWAKE is first sighted:
The horn at the fort changes. Two short notes. Repeated. You know that signal. It means: they see it too. It means Alard knows the clock just shortened.

BEAT 4 — If the party stops BLACKWAKE:
The horn at the fort sounds differently. Three notes. A pause. Three again. You have never heard that signal before. A soldier beside you exhales. "That's the breakthrough signal. He broke through." A pause. "He actually broke through."

SIR ALARD — WALL OUTCOME (DM Reference)

Regardless of party action at the docks, Sir Alard holds the wall. The cost:
- He takes a wound in the final push — not fatal, but visible. He will carry it.
- Twelve soldiers under his command die in the breach. He knows their names.
- The officer who tried to scapegoat the party during Floor 2 dies on the wall. Alard does not mention this. The soldiers do.

If the party stops BLACKWAKE, Alard breaks the siege from inside when the horde's rear falls into confusion. The wall holds.

If the party does not stop BLACKWAKE in time, the horde lands troops at the bay. The wall is flanked. Alard executes the civilian evacuation doctrine and retreats — alive, but the fort falls.

DM NOTE: Both outcomes still end the floor. Victory requires both the wall holding AND BLACKWAKE stopped. The floor's tone depends on the party making the hard call.

BLOCK 3 — THE DOCK ROAD
Two Miles. Forty Soldiers. One Hour.

DM READ-ALOUD: The March

The dock road runs south along the river, away from the wall.

Every soldier in your division knows they are marching away from the fight everyone else is running toward.

No one argues. No one asks questions.

They trust the people who came back from the swamp.

Some of them were there when the proof landed on the war table. They know what you saw. They know what's coming.

Unit Size: 40 soldiers total
Veterans: 12 — soldiers who served under Alard, disciplined, steady
Conscripts: 20 — local militia, trained for eight months, reliable under leadership
Specialists: 8 — crossbowmen, one field surgeon, two engineers
Morale: High — they chose this assignment. They know what it means.
Commander: The party — orders are followed without question, but soldiers watch for hesitation

BLOCK 4 — THE DOCKS
Thirty Minutes Before BLACKWAKE

DM READ-ALOUD: Arrival at the Bay Mouth

The dock road ends at the bay mouth.

Two stone platforms flank the channel — circular towers at their centers, fire pits burning, torches already lit along the bridge that connects them.

Someone was here before you. Alard's bay watchers. They prepared what they could and then they ran.

The platforms are intact. The bridge holds.

But the fog to the south is the wrong color.

Too orange. Too warm. Moving wrong.

That is not fog. That is smoke from whatever BLACKWAKE already burned on its way in.

And through it, just visible — the sound of oars. Rhythmic. Purposeful. Getting closer.

Behind you, one of the veteran soldiers says quietly:

"We are not enough."

A pause.

"Tell us what to do anyway."

THE THREE PHASES — MAP OVERVIEW

PHASE 1 (Rounds 1-2): Bay Mouth — Two Chain Towers, Central Bridge
Party establishes positions. Scout boats probe the bridge. BLACKWAKE is in the smoke to the south. The bay chain is the only thing that might slow it.

PHASE 2 (Rounds 3-5): Channel Entry — BLACKWAKE Under Oar
The galley-ironclad enters the channel. Bolt throwers open fire on both platforms simultaneously. Red canvas and timber burn. The chain can still engage — but the window is closing.

PHASE 3 (Round 6+): Town Plaza — The Ram
BLACKWAKE does not anchor. Does not deploy ramps. It drives its bow through the stone pier and beaches in the plaza. The ship IS the landing ramp. Marines walk off the bow into the town square.

PHASE 1 TERRAIN

West Chain Tower: Stone, circular. Fire pit burning at center. Chain mechanism corroded — needs Engineering. BOTH towers required to raise bay chain. Crossbowmen here have elevated fire lanes.

East Chain Tower: Mirror of west. BOTH must be activated simultaneously — requires splitting party or dividing engineers.

Central Bridge: BLACKWAKE's scout boats will push marines across this in Round 2. Veterans hold it — without them, it falls immediately.

Harbormaster's Building: Stone, northwest. Will not burn. Best position for field surgeon and wounded.

Red Canvas Market Stalls: Flammable. BLACKWAKE's bolt throwers will ignite these in Phase 2. Do not stage soldiers here.

Timber Dock Extensions: Will burn in Phase 2. Any soldiers on outer dock arms need to pull back.

PHASE 1 — PREPARATION (One Planning Round)

Ask the party out loud: BLACKWAKE is coming through that channel. You have two chain towers, one on each side. You have forty soldiers. Where does everyone go?

Every choice made here has a direct mechanical consequence in Phases 2 and 3:
- Engineers to both chain towers (split): Bay chain becomes available in Phase 2.
- Crossbowmen to chain towers: +2 to hit against BLACKWAKE deck crew in Phase 2.
- Veterans on the central bridge: Bridge holds for 3 rounds against scout boat marines.
- Field surgeon to harbormaster's building: One soldier who would die in Phase 3 stabilizes.
- Splitting party across both platforms: Better coverage, but neither position has full party strength.
- Keeping party together on one platform: One side undefended when Phase 2 bolt fire begins.

BLOCK 5 — BLACKWAKE ARRIVES
The Ship That Should Not Exist

DM READ-ALOUD: Contact

It comes out of the morning fog like something the world forgot to forbid.

Slow. Inevitable. Steam rising from its iron flanks.

The acid resin coating catches the light — the hull does not rust. Does not corrode.

It is exactly as the schematic said it would be. Bigger than you remembered.

Three hundred feet of iron hull. Two crane-towers of its own. Ramp mechanisms on the bow.

And aboard it, disciplined and ready, an assault force that has been waiting a year for this bay.

The BLACKWAKE slows as it approaches anchorage.

From the command deck, a figure in a long riveted coat looks toward the docks.

Even at this distance, you recognize the goggles.

Vexrath Sablegear sees you too.

He taps his cane once against the rail.

He does not look surprised. He looks... interested.

BLACKWAKE — SIEGE CONSTRUCT (Gargantuan Object)

Hull AC: 18 (iron-reinforced, acid resin coated — immune to fire, resistant to piercing)
Hull HP: 180 — Threshold: at 90 HP oars destroyed and cannot steer; at 0 hull breaches and begins sinking over 3 rounds
Speed: 40 ft. water (oar-powered, does not need wind)
Crew: 40 Iron Marines, 8 Bolt Thrower Crew, Vexrath Sablegear (boss)

Bolt Throwers (x2): +6 to hit, range 60/200 ft., 4d10 piercing. Each thrower targets structures or groups of 3+ creatures in a 10 ft. area.

Acid Hull Aura: Any creature starting its turn touching the hull takes 1d6 acid damage. Climbing requires DC 13 Athletics each round or 2d6 acid.

Ram Prow: On the round it beaches (Phase 3), all creatures in a 30 ft. cone ahead of the bow must make DC 15 Str save or take 4d10 bludgeoning and be knocked prone.

Oar Banks: AC 12, HP 20 each side. Destroying one oar bank reduces speed to 20 ft. Destroying both halts all water movement.

- Pack Tactics: Advantage on attack rolls if ally within 5 ft. of target.

VEXRATH — MANNER IN COMBAT

He does not rage. He does not taunt. He assesses out loud, as if thinking through a problem. He is disappointed by inefficiency. He is genuinely curious when the party surprises him.

"Interesting. Both towers. You split the activation."
"I had accounted for one."
He looks at the chain cutting across the channel.
"I had not accounted for coordination under fire."

VEXRATH — SURRENDER CONDITION

When BLACKWAKE's hull reaches 0 HP AND Vexrath is below 25 HP, he stops fighting. Not from fear — from calculation.

He lowers the cane.
"The ship is done."
A pause. He does not look at the wreck.
"You stopped the design. Not the intent."
"I remember faces."
He steps off the hull into the water. He does not surface where you can see.

DM NOTE: Vexrath is a recurring antagonist. He survives intentionally. If the party tries to pursue him into the water, the acid resin makes the hull impassable and the bay is deep. He is gone. He said he would build another.

BLOCK 6 — THE FIGHT AT THE DOCKS
Three Phases. Nothing Stops It. Win Anyway.

PHASE 2 — BLACKWAKE ENTERS THE CHANNEL

DM READ-ALOUD:

It comes out of the smoke the way something comes out of a nightmare.
Not fast. Not loud. Just... present. Then closer. Then undeniable.

BLACKWAKE fills the channel the way a landslide fills a valley — not quickly, but completely.

Iron hull. Oar banks working in perfect rhythm. Two bolt throwers on the deck already pivoting toward your positions.

The acid resin on the hull catches the dawn light and does not reflect it. It absorbs it.

On both sides of the channel, the market stalls begin to burn. Not from fire you set. From bolts you didn't hear coming.

And on the command deck, walking the hull like a man on a morning inspection, the black dragonborn looks toward the towers.

He sees you. He taps his cane once. Then he goes back to watching the channel.

PHASE 2 ROUND-BY-ROUND:

Round 1: BLACKWAKE enters channel. West bolt thrower fires on west chain tower (4d10). East bolt thrower fires on bridge soldiers. Bay chain activation attempt (both towers must act). Bridge veterans: DC 13 Con save or 1 killed.

Round 2: Moves 40 ft. closer. Vexrath uses Controlled Burst on whichever tower activated first. Best round to destroy an oar bank (AC 12, HP 20).

Round 3: Moves 40 ft. — now at the chain tower line. If chain raised: 3d10 bludgeoning, speed 0 for 1 round, hull AC drops to 16 for 2 rounds. If chain not raised: ship passes through.

Round 4: If stopped by chain — Vexrath uses Acid Breath on closest chain tower (DC 14, 8d6 acid). Marines on scout boats try to board the bridge. If not stopped: Phase 3 begins.

THE BAY CHAIN (Special Option)

Requires engineers assigned to BOTH chain towers during Phase 1, OR two party members each at a tower making checks simultaneously.

Investigation (Int) DC 14: Understand the mechanism. Success reduces Engineering DC by 2.
Tinker's Tools / Athletics DC 16: Crank the chain housing. Both towers must succeed on the same round.
Athletics (Str) DC 13: Manual override if mechanism jams mid-crank.

If BOTH towers succeed same round: chain rises, BLACKWAKE takes 3d10 bludgeoning, speed 0 for 1 round, hull AC 16 for 2 rounds.
If only ONE tower succeeds: partial chain — ship slows to 20 ft. but not stopped. Buys one extra round.
If ship already past towers when chain rises: chain snaps on hull — 2d10 damage, no stopping effect, mechanism destroyed.

PHASE 3 — BLACKWAKE BEACHES

DM READ-ALOUD:

You expect it to slow.
Ships slow. Ships anchor. Ships wait for the tide.
BLACKWAKE does not slow.

The bow hits the stone pier at full oar-speed.

The sound is not a crash. It is a statement.

Cobblestones explode upward. The fountain plaza erupts with shrapnel and displaced stone.

The bow punches through the pier and buries itself in the plaza.

And then the oars stop.

And the bow ramp folds down.

The ramp is the landing zone.

There are no gates to close. No water to cross.

There is only forty feet of iron bow between you and everything that has been waiting to kill you.

And at the top of that ramp, walking down it with the ease of a man descending his own front steps — Vexrath Sablegear.

RAM IMPACT: Every creature in a 30 ft. cone ahead of the bow makes DC 15 Str save. Fail: 4d10 bludgeoning, knocked prone, pushed 15 ft. Success: half damage, not prone. Creatures behind full cover are unaffected.

PHASE 3 TERRAIN:

BLACKWAKE Bow (Beached): 40 ft. of acid-coated iron hull. Cannot be moved.
Rubble Field: 30 ft. radius around bow. Difficult terrain. Half cover if prone.
Fountain/Garden (Center Plaza): Cracked fountain, half cover. Trees burning by Phase 3 Round 2.
Burning Market Stalls (NE): Adjacent creatures take 1d6 fire. Explodes Round 3 — 3d6 fire, 10 ft. radius, DC 13 Dex save.
Left Dock Buildings (NW): Stone construction. Full cover. Field surgeon staged here if assigned.
Bow Ramp: 40 ft. long, 10 ft. wide. Marines descend 5 per round.

PHASE 3 ENCOUNTER COMPOSITION:

Wave 1 (Round 1): 5 Iron Marines descend ramp.
Wave 2 (Round 2): 5 more Iron Marines. Vexrath in combat. Bolt thrower crew fires into plaza.
Wave 3 (Round 3): 5 more Iron Marines. Burning stall explosion check. Wall horn not yet.
Wave 4 (Round 4+): 5 more Iron Marines per round. Alard's horn sounds at end of Round 4 or 5.

STOPPING THE MARINE FLOW:

Destroy the ramp (AC 14, HP 40): Ramp collapses — remaining marines cannot deploy. Vexrath loses his Reaction for 1 round.
Hold the ramp top (melee): Maximum 2 marines can attack the position at once. Extremely dangerous. Extremely effective.
Acid Barrel Detonation (if carried from Floor 2): 6d6 acid to all creatures on ramp, jams mechanism for 3 rounds.

VICTORY CONDITIONS (all required):
- BLACKWAKE's hull at 0 HP
- Vexrath surrendered or driven into the water
- Fewer than 5 Iron Marines remain standing
- Alard's three-note horn has sounded

IF THE PARTY IS STRUGGLING (use one, never all three):
1. Kobold engineer (if captured Floor 2): climbs hull, finds failsafe, activates it joyfully. BLACKWAKE detonates from inside — 6d10 force in 20 ft. radius, hull immediately 0 HP. The kobold is in the blast.
2. Structural weakness — the seam Vexrath had redone in the swamp: DC 14 Investigation to locate. Three attacks in one round totaling 30+ damage causes cascade hull failure. Hull AC drops to 12 for 2 rounds.
3. Alard's cavalry arrives early (Round 3): 8 mounted soldiers crash through north plaza, cutting off marine reinforcements.

BLOCK 8 — THE AFTERMATH
Victory, Costly But Real

DM READ-ALOUD:

The bay is quiet.

BLACKWAKE's iron superstructure sits at the bottom with the grace of everything that was built to last and wasn't supposed to sink.

The horde has broken at the wall.

Not routed — the orcs do not rout easily — but the coherent assault is over.

Fragments pull back into the treeline. Organized pursuit begins at dawn.

On the loading platform, your soldiers are sitting down.

Some are tending wounds. Some are staring at the water.

One of them starts laughing — not hysteria, just the specific kind of disbelief that comes after something that wasn't supposed to work works.

It spreads.

Not celebration. Just the sound of people who are still alive and didn't expect to be.

SIR ALARD — ARRIVAL

Alard reaches the docks within the hour, on horseback, covered in someone else's blood and walking slightly wrong.

He says nothing for a long moment.

Then: "How many?"

He means the dead. He will carry the number with every other number he carries.

He looks at the wreck in the bay.

"He'll build another," Alard says. He is not wrong.

"But not here. Not today."

A pause.

"Today, we held."

THE COST:
- Name the soldiers who died. Even just one or two, specifically.
- The fort sustained significant damage — it will hold, but needs months of repair.
- The village Alard protected during Floor 2 survived — scouts confirm it is intact.
- Alard's wound is real. He will not say how serious. He will not sit down.

KARMA AWARDS:

Choosing the docks over the wall — the smart and harder play: 50-100
Stopping BLACKWAKE before full marine landing: 100-200
Destroying both landing ramps: 25-50
Using the bay chain (especially if engineered under fire): 50-75
Division survivors above 25 soldiers: 50-100
Confronting Vexrath directly in combat: 25-75
Moral weight — acknowledging the cost of the victory: 25-50

BLOCK 9 — THE TOWER PULLS THEM
The Floor Ends Before the Celebration Does

DM READ-ALOUD:

It happens the way it always does.

Not with a warning. Not with a goodbye.

The world begins to soften at the edges.

The uniform threads start to loosen. The military coat relaxes. The insignia fades.

You have been here long enough. The Tower has seen what it needed to see.

You have time for one last look.

Alard stands at the dock's edge, watching the bay.

He does not look back.

But he raises one hand.

Not a wave.

A salute.

Given to people behind him. Given to you.

The docks disappear.

The cold air disappears.

The smell of iron and acid and blood disappears.

What does not disappear: the knowledge that it held.

The war was not over. But this part of it — this day, this bay, this wall — held.
