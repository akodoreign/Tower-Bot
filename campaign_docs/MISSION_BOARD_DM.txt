MISSION BOARD
DM Reference — Full Mission Details

Current Posting Period

This document contains full DM-facing mission details for every contract on the current board. Each entry includes the true situation, key NPCs, complications, failure consequences, and hooks into longer faction arcs.

Missions are organised by faction, then standing. Personal missions are clearly marked. No mission on this board represents an immediate or time-critical emergency — they are background jobs available between floors. If a Rift breakout or floor incursion occurs, the emergency board replaces these.

DM Standing Rule: If the party falls below Neutral with any faction, that faction's missions become invisible to them until standing recovers. Do not announce this — the board simply will not have those contracts when they look.

THE GLASS SIGIL
Standing: Friendly | Base: Sanctum Quarter / Grand Forum | Leader: Senior Archivist Pell

The Glass Sigil are the Tower's unofficial archivists — arcanist scholars who catalogue Rift residue, relics, and magical anomalies that the major guilds do not consider worth tracking. They are precise, underfunded, and quietly indispensable. At Friendly standing, they treat the party as field partners rather than contract labour. Pell in particular has noted the party's Floor track record with interest.

--- MISSION: Echoes in the Residue ---
Type: Investigation / Recovery
Reward: 80 EC + Sigil Ink Vial (identifies magical residue, 3 charges)
Location: The Warrens — eastern alley cluster near the Collapsed Plaza
Time Window: Available until end of posting period. Residue degrades over about 2 weeks.
Contact: Junior Archivist Dova, stationed at the Warrens entrance

Full Situation: A minor Rift sealed itself approximately six days ago in an alley off the Warrens. Dova noticed that the residue left behind has an unusual crystalline structure she has never seen in previous surveys. She flagged it to Pell, who authorised collection before Mara the Scrapper's crew finds it and melts it down for scrap coin.

The residue itself is non-hostile but reactive. Strong emotional states within 10 feet of the crystals cause them to pulse faintly and emit a harmonic tone. The Sigil does not fully understand why. That is why they want the sample.

Complications: Mara's crew already knows about the residue. Two of her scrappers have been seen in the area. This is not a combat situation — Mara has no particular beef with the party — but she will want a cut or an explanation if she catches them collecting from what she considers her territory.

Additionally: the tone the crystals emit is not random. Dova suspects it is a frequency echo of whatever came through the Rift. She will pay a bonus (10 EC + note in their file) if the party can describe what emotion or action triggered the resonance during collection.

DM NOTE: Low-stakes, flavour-rich mission that rewards careful roleplay. The crystals are a setup — the unusual Rift signature will resurface in a later posting or Rift event. Dova is a recurring NPC.

HOOK: The crystal's resonance matches a harmonic that Dova has only seen in records of much larger Rift events — specifically, events that preceded major floor destabilisations. She does not tell the party this yet.

--- MISSION: The Misfiled Relic ---
Type: Retrieval / Social
Reward: 60 EC + Favour Token (redeemable: one free archive query, no questions asked)
Location: Adventurer's Inn, Grand Forum
Time Window: Available. Wex has been here a week; he will not stay forever.
Contact: Archivist Dova can brief on the item; Pell wants it handled quietly

Full Situation: The item in question is a small iron disc engraved with a partial Rift map — low-tier, non-functional, but academically significant because it predates the current Tower epoch. Courier Wex was paid to deliver it to the Sigil. He did not deliver it. He has been drinking at the Adventurer's Inn for a week.

Wex is not a villain. He opened the package, recognised that what was inside was probably worth more than his delivery fee, got frightened by that realisation, and has been paralysed by indecision ever since. He still has the disc. He does not want to be in trouble. He is in moderate trouble.

Negotiation: Wex will not hand over the disc to anyone who seems there to punish him. He responds to honesty, sympathy, and practical assurances. Will the Sigil pursue action against him? No, if the disc is returned intact. Can he keep his courier licence? Yes, if he is discreet about what he saw. Is the disc dangerous? No — and this is true. If the party offers him 10 EC of their own coin as a good faith gesture, he hands it over immediately and buys them a drink.

DM NOTE: Pure social mission. No combat. Wex is a recurring face if treated well — he is a courier with Warrens connections who now owes the party a mild favour.

HOOK: The disc's Rift map matches part of a grid Dova has been trying to piece together for two months. She does not know the party has seen it yet — but when she finds out, she will ask to interview them.

★ Catalogue Escort
Type: Escort / Investigation (light)

But they do not know that yet.

HOOK: The relic the Lotus is looking for is in a different Sigil vault entirely. If the party ever investigates further, they will find the Lotus has been quietly surveilling the Sigil for three months. The Sigil does not know. Pell will want to know.

WARDENS OF ASH
Standing: Favorable | Base: Outer Wall and Gates | Leader: Captain Havel Korin

The Wardens are the Tower's disciplined defenders — militarised, rule-bound, and deeply serious about the city's security. At Favorable standing, they treat the party as reliable contractors. Captain Korin specifically has filed a commendation for the party's Floor 3 conduct that circulated internally.

--- MISSION: Sewer Sweep — Grid Seven ---
Type: Patrol / Rift Clearance (light)
Reward: 75 EC + Warden Supply Chit (redeemable: standard field kit, healing potion, or one repair service)
Location: Sewer grid seven — accessible via Outer Wall maintenance tunnel B
Time Window: Available. Readings climb slowly. No urgency yet — but "yet" is doing work there.
Contact: Lieutenant Varen at Outer Wall post. She provides monitoring equipment and map.

Full Situation: Rift residue monitors in grid seven have been showing elevated readings for four days. Consistent with a slow bleed — not a breach, not a creature, but an accumulation of raw Rift energy seeping through a crack in the tunnel infrastructure. Left alone for another week or two, it could become a minor breach. Mapped and reported now, the Wardens can seal it before that.

The sweep is a two-person job with provided instruments. The party navigates approximately 200 metres of sewer tunnel, takes readings at six marked points, identifies the source location, and returns.

Complications: The sewer also contains a small Obsidian Lotus dead-drop point in grid seven — specifically in junction D4. The party will find it if they are thorough. Inside: a sealed message container, currently full. The Lotus has been using it because the Wardens have always been too focused on the residue readings to notice the side tunnel. Options: report to Varen (Warden standing increase, Lotus standing decrease), take the message (illegal, risky, informative), ignore it (probably wise, definitely boring), or leave a note of their own inside (chaotic, interesting, may cause problems later).

DM NOTE: Low combat risk. Medium investigation interest. Varen is a good recurring Warden contact: professional, precise, fair.

HOOK: The residue accumulation in grid seven is connected to the same unusual Rift signature from the Glass Sigil's crystal mission. The party will not know this yet. Dova will figure it out eventually.

--- PERSONAL MISSION — Posted by: Captain Havel Korin ---
★ Find Recruit Aldric
Type: Investigation / Personal
Reward: 90 EC + Warden Field Kit (healing potion x2, rope, signal flare, rations) + Standing Increase
Location: Last seen near Warrens entrance. Trail likely runs through Warrens interior.
Time Window: Korin wants this resolved before he is forced to file officially. That is 3 days from board posting.
Contact: Korin in person — he will brief privately, not at the duty station.

Full Situation: Recruit Aldric Vess is nineteen, two months into his Warden service, doing well by all accounts. Not the type to desert. Korin knows his people, and he knows Aldric would not walk without a reason.

The reason: Aldric's younger sister Mira is in the Warrens, and she was recruited by Brother Thane's cult three weeks ago. Aldric went looking for her on his own time, found her, tried to talk her out. She refused. He went back a second time. Thane noticed. On his third visit, Thane's people told him plainly that if he came back with anyone official, Mira would be "relocated to a place of deeper commitment." Aldric believes them. He has been hiding near the Cult House ever since, watching, not knowing what to do.

He is not in danger. He is not defecting. He is a scared kid who got in over his head trying to protect his sister.

Finding Aldric: Investigation DC 13 (street contacts, Warrens locals) identifies a young man in Warden-adjacent clothing sleeping rough near Shantytown Heights. He will bolt if approached by anyone in uniform. He will stay if the party approaches without Warden colours visible and makes clear they are not there to arrest him. Once found, he will tell them everything. He wants help — he just did not know how to ask. He will ask the party to help get Mira out. That is a separate job (see Patchwork Saints: Brother Thane Is Recruiting Again) but the threads connect.

DM NOTE: This mission threads directly into the Patchwork Saints personal mission. Aldric is a recurring NPC if rescued well — he is loyal, capable, and will remember. Korin's investigation into Thane is a slow-burn consequence; it will not resolve this posting period but plants stakes for future ones.

HOOK: Mira Vess is not a simple cult victim — she went to Thane voluntarily because she had a vision she believes was divine. Whether that vision was genuine, manufactured by Thane, or something else entirely is up to the DM.

PATCHWORK SAINTS
Standing: Favorable | Base: The Warrens | Leader: (referred to as "the Leader")

The Patchwork Saints are failed adventurers who chose to stay in the Warrens and protect the people there. They operate on pure principle and minimal resources. At Favorable standing, they treat the party as fellow travellers — not contractors. They ask for help because they trust you, not because they can pay well.

--- MISSION: Supply Run — Shantytown Heights ---
Type: Escort / Delivery
Reward: 50 EC + Saints' Safe Passage Marker (once: the Warrens will leave you alone)
Location: Elune's Apothecary to Shantytown Heights via Collapsed Plaza or alternate route
Time Window: The medicine is time-sensitive (fever outbreak, three families affected). 5 days.
Contact: Saints member Brek at Elune's picks up the shipment and can accompany if wanted.

Full Situation: Shantytown Heights has a fever outbreak — three families, eight people total, one child critical. Elune has prepared the medication. Getting it there is the problem. The Collapsed Plaza, the direct route, has been "taxed" by three independent scavenger crews shaking down couriers for a week. The Saints do not have the numbers to clear the route and do not want a war with the scavengers.

The scavenger leader, Garrett, is supporting about twelve dependents. He is aggressive but not stupid. He knows the Saints. He knows this is medicine. If the party explains clearly, he will let them through — he just needs to not lose face in front of his crew.

Alternate Route: A longer path through Echo Alley that avoids the Plaza. Takes twice as long. The alley is acoustically haunted — old Rift sounds echo there permanently. No combat threat but Wisdom DC 12 saves to avoid mild disorientation. Safe, just unpleasant.

DM NOTE: This mission is about judgment, not combat. If the party clears the scavengers violently, it creates a minor Warrens reputation hit. Getting through via negotiation earns more goodwill than any amount of fighting.

HOOK: Garrett's crew have been taxing the route specifically because they intercepted a rumour that something valuable was moving through it soon. The rumour was planted — by whom and why is a future thread.

--- MISSION: The Night Pits Debt ---
Type: Investigation / Negotiation
Reward: 65 EC + Patchwork Marker (Warrens passage)
Location: The Night Pits, Warrens
Time Window: Doss has four days before the pit boss sends collectors.
Contact: Doss himself near the Warren Bazaar; Saints member Calla provides background.

Full Situation: Doss is a 50-year-old former dockworker who placed a 30 EC bet on a pit fight based on insider information that turned out to be wrong. He owes 90 EC. He has 12. The pit boss, Sable, runs a clean-ish operation — she does not want to hurt Doss, but she cannot let the debt stand publicly or everyone will stop paying.

Options: Negotiate a payment plan (Sable will accept 30 EC now, 30 EC in two weeks, 30 EC in a month — she is reasonable if approached respectfully). Offer the party's own labour as partial settlement (Sable always needs people she can trust for one-night security jobs). Find out who gave Doss the bad tip and bring that person to Sable. Or simply pay Doss's debt — he will repay slowly and loyally over several posting periods.

Who Gave the Bad Tip: The inside information came from a Saints-adjacent figure who genuinely believed it was accurate. They got the information from someone at the Night Pits working a longer con — manipulating several bets simultaneously. That person has already left the Pits. Sable knows they were played too. If the party surfaces this, Sable's anger redirects entirely away from Doss.

DM NOTE: Sable is a well-run minor antagonist — firm, fair, not evil. Treat her with respect. Doss as a grateful recurring NPC is worth more than the 65 EC reward long-term.

--- PERSONAL MISSION — Posted by: The Patchwork Saints Leader ---
★ Brother Thane Is Recruiting Again
Type: Surveillance / Disruption
Reward: 80 EC + Standing Increase (Favorable to Trusted) + Saints Voucher (call on Saints assistance once)
Location: Brother Thane's Cult House, Warrens; street recruitment near Collapsed Plaza
Time Window: Open. Thane has been running this for months. The Saints want it addressed before it gets worse.
Contact: The Leader in person — will meet the party at the Warren Bazaar.

Full Situation: Brother Thane has been pulling vulnerable people — mostly young, mostly desperate — from the Heights and the Collapsed Plaza district for three months. The Saints have lost at least six people they were watching over. They need outside help.

The Leader wants two things: intelligence (who is going in, can they be reached, how many are still persuadable) and disruption (breaking up at least one recruitment session publicly, demonstrating that Thane does not own this street).

Surveillance (Days 1-2): Stealth DC 12 to avoid being noticed by Thane's sentries. Investigation DC 14 over 4+ hours to build a useful picture of traffic in and out. The party will observe about 20 residents currently committed, another 10-15 who attend meetings but have not gone internal. Of the internal members, at least four came in under duress or manipulation and could be talked out under the right circumstances.

Thane is aware of surveillance. He does not stop it — he uses it. Anyone watching for too long will be invited in. The invitation feels warm. It is not warm.

The Disruption: Thane runs street recruitment sessions near the Collapsed Plaza at dusk, three times a week. Disrupting one session: Performance DC 14 or Persuasion DC 13 to pull at least three people away. Can expose one cultist as a known Thane operative (Investigation DC 13). Can simply stand there visibly and make the recruits uncomfortable enough to leave — Thane's people will not escalate in public because that would draw Warden attention.

Thane's Response: If handled without violence, Thane does nothing immediately. He files it away. The party will start appearing in his dreams — which is unsettling, and which he finds theologically interesting. If the party is violent or causes a scene, Thane files a Warden complaint (legitimately — he has legal standing as a "religious community organization"). The Wardens will investigate the party, not Thane.

DM NOTE: This mission threads directly into the Wardens "Find Recruit Aldric" mission — Aldric's sister Mira is in the Cult House. The party may find her during surveillance. Do not force the connection; let them discover it. Thane is a long-burn antagonist, not a boss fight. He should never be easy to dismiss or remove cleanly.

HOOK: Thane's visions are real. Something is communicating through him. It is not the god he believes it is. He is genuinely devout and genuinely deceived. That makes him more dangerous, not less.

ARGENT BLADES
Glory-driven adventurer guild. They would sponsor the party — if the party gave them the story rights.

--- MISSION: The Missing Champion ---
Type: Investigation
Reward: 85 EC + Public Recognition (Minor — a Blades herald will name-drop the party at the next arena event)
Location: Trail starts at the Guild Spires, runs through Markets Infinite
Time Window: The arena bout is in 6 days. After that, the mission expires.
Contact: Blades manager Darro, Guild Spires.

Full Situation: Orro Senn is one of the Blades' mid-tier arena fighters — skilled, popular, solid earner. He disappeared four days before a bout that has significant coin riding on it. His sparring partner Tev saw two people approach Orro after practice three days ago. He did not hear the conversation. Orro went pale. He left with them.

Orro is being held — not violently, but in the way that a very firm conversation about a very large debt creates a kind of voluntary captivity. Resolving It: Investigation DC 13 (Markets Infinite contacts) locates the crew within a few hours. Options: pay the debt (200 EC), expose the lender to Blades management — Darro will cover the debt in exchange for a public corruption story — or negotiate a fight-outcome arrangement that technically satisfies the lender without Orro throwing the bout (Deception DC 15, risky).

DM NOTE: The Lotus thread here is deliberately minor — a freelance lender using Lotus-adjacent methods, not an official Lotus operation. The "public recognition" reward is genuinely useful — a Blades herald mentioning the party's name at the arena raises their profile in ways that compound over time.

OBSIDIAN LOTUS
Black-market syndicate. They test new contractors with clean jobs. Watch how the party handles this.

--- MISSION: Quiet Delivery ---
Type: Courier / Stealth
Reward: 70 EC (cash, no record)
Location: Pickup: specified alley in Warrens. Delivery: Sanctum Quarter, contact confirms by signal.
Time Window: Job window: 3 hours from confirmation. Lotus will confirm 30 minutes before.
Contact: Lotus contact "Hess" — never seen in full light, leaves messages at Warren Bazaar.

Full Situation: The Lotus is testing the party. This is their standard first contract for new contractors — a clean job with no obvious illegality, designed to see how the party handles instructions, discretion, and unexpected complications.

The container holds: three sealed letters for a Serpent Choir deacon who has been quietly exchanging information with Lotus intermediaries. The letters contain financial records that the Choir does not want circulating. This is not illegal under Tower law. It is politically sensitive. The Choir knows the letters are moving — they commissioned the delivery themselves through a cutout, which the party does not know.

The "unexpected complication": a Warden patrol crosses their path on the Sanctum Quarter approach. The patrol is routine; the Wardens have no interest in the party specifically. But the party does not know that. How they handle a potential confrontation while carrying a mysterious sealed container tells the Lotus something useful.

If the Container Is Opened: The letters are in cipher. Decoding requires Arcana DC 16 or Intelligence DC 15 (takes 10 minutes, requires tools). If decoded, the party learns about the Choir-Lotus information exchange — useful intelligence that at least two factions would pay well for. Opening the container also voids the Lotus contract and starts a different kind of conversation with Hess.

DM NOTE: The Lotus will not punish the party for opening the container — they will just note that this contractor does not follow instructions. Future Lotus missions will be more complex and better supervised. The Choir-Lotus exchange is a genuine political thread worth developing.

HOOK: The Serpent Choir deacon who receives these letters is the same individual who authorised the Choir's missing tithe ledger mission. He has been playing both sides of a very uncomfortable fence for eight months.

SERPENT CHOIR
Divine contract brokers. They do not advertise their problems. This one is bad enough that they had to.

--- MISSION: Recover the Tithe Ledger ---
Type: Retrieval / Investigation
Reward: 80 EC + Choir Blessing (Minor, 1 use — advantage on one save, or +2 to one skill check, declared before rolling)
Location: Trail starts at the Grand Forum, last known movement toward Markets Infinite
Time Window: Six days since Sevas went missing. Financial records degrade in value daily.
Contact: Choir Liaison Brother Enn, Grand Forum Choir office — formal, terse, will not answer questions about Sevas personally.

Full Situation: Sevas was not a courier. She was a mid-tier Choir financial officer — technically a broker, practically a keeper of secrets. The ledger she was carrying does not just list tithe amounts; it records what specific donations were exchanged for in terms of divine favours, contract clauses, and memory transactions. It is, in a word, compromising.

Sevas disappeared voluntarily. She is not dead, not kidnapped, and not in danger — she walked away and took the ledger with her. She is currently hiding in a rented room in the Floating Bazaar district, trying to decide what to do with what she knows.

What she knows: the Choir has been running an undisclosed sub-operation that skims a percentage of divine favour transactions and redirects it to a fund that Brother Enn controls personally. Sevas found the records by accident three weeks ago. She took the ledger as insurance. She has not gone to anyone yet because she does not know who to trust.

Finding Her: Investigation DC 14 (Grand Forum contacts, market district trail) locates Sevas within a day. She will see the party coming. First conversation: she is scared, defensive, and will deny having the ledger. She opens up if the party demonstrates they are not Choir-directed, or if they admit they were but express personal doubt about the mission.

She wants the ledger to reach someone who can actually use it. She will hand it over willingly if the party agrees to deliver it to either the Ashen Scrolls Guild or Magister Liora at the Tower Authority — not the Choir. If they promise to return it to the Choir, she will refuse and disappear again.

The Choice:
- Return the ledger to the Choir as instructed: 80 EC, Choir blessing, Sevas is "handled" by the Choir, Brother Enn's operation continues.
- Deliver it to the Scrolls or Liora as Sevas requests: no Choir payment, potential reward from Scrolls/Liora, Choir standing drops to Unfriendly, Brother Enn's operation unravels over the next several months.
- Keep the ledger: very risky, very valuable, makes three parties unhappy simultaneously.

DM NOTE: This is the most ethically complex mission on this board. Do not push the party toward any outcome — let them make the call. Brother Enn is the same deacon from the Lotus delivery hook. The Choir's internal corruption thread is long-burn; even if the party hands the ledger back, Sevas is still out there and still knows what she knows.

HOOK: If the ledger reaches Magister Liora, she will open a Tower Authority investigation. This takes months. It will surface during a future posting period at the worst possible moment for the Choir — and for anyone who has made deals with them recently.

DM THREADING NOTE

This posting period contains a quiet through-line: an unusual Rift signature appearing in four separate missions — the Glass Sigil crystal residue, the Wardens' sewer grid, the Iron Fang vendor's seam, and the Guild's wall survey anomaly. None of these missions make the connection explicit. A party that completes multiple jobs may notice the pattern.
