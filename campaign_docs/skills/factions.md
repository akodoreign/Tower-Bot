## Major Factions

The Undercity is ruled not by a single government but by a web of competing factions. Each has its own territory, agenda, and style.

### The Wardens
The closest thing the Undercity has to law enforcement. Military-structured, duty-bound. They patrol the Outer Wall, maintain order in the Grand Forum, and run the city's defensive infrastructure. Sub-groups include The Quiet Majority (covert operatives) and the Blacksmiths (led by Gruum Boneshaper).

### Iron Fang Consortium
The dominant trade cartel. Controls supply chains, import/export licenses, and black-market luxury goods. Led by Guildmaster Serrik Dhal. Operates primarily out of Cobbleway Market and the Guild Spires.

### Crestmark Advance
Elite negotiators and dealmakers. They handle contracts, arbitration, and high-value trade. Known for adaptability and political cunning. Base in the Floating Bazaar.

### Gravelight Company
Mercenary crew specializing in dangerous jobs — rift expeditions, bounty hunting, escort work. Respected for competence, feared for ruthlessness.

### The Unmarked Hours
A shadowy faction operating in the margins. Rumored to deal in secrets, assassination contracts, and intelligence brokering.

### Obsidian Lotus
The Undercity's premier black-market syndicate. Deals in contraband, forbidden magic components, and stolen artifacts.

## Faction Reputation System

Player characters can gain or lose reputation with factions through missions, roleplay, and downtime activities. Reputation is tracked in tiers that unlock benefits (or consequences) with each faction.

## Reading Faction Dynamics

When players ask about faction relations, the Oracle should reference actual recent events from the news feed rather than generic descriptions. Factions are living, breathing organizations whose relationships shift based on what happens in play.
